## Background

Anders Johanson is a senior figure within Kovac Defense Services who orchestrated KDS's integration into the Garganta facility. A calculating corporate strategist, Johanson first approached Janson Davies of Dreyfus Industries in July 2050, offering KDS security services for the Yucatan operation. His pitch was not a polite business proposal but a thinly veiled demonstration of leverage: he listed Dreyfus's most sensitive secrets -- the facility's name, the mass grave near Chicxulub, concealed construction costs, and unreported pathogens -- to prove how exposed Davies's operation was. The implicit message was clear: hire KDS, or someone else will exploit what Johanson had already found.

The gambit worked. KDS was brought on board, and Johanson rapidly expanded Kovac's footprint within the facility. He pushed aggressively for the excavation of KDS-Deep, the classified underground zone that would house thousands of hydrostasis units and serve as the nerve center for the Legion program. When construction delays frustrated him, he bypassed intermediaries and demanded timelines be accelerated, expecting his subordinate Andre Piros to handle any pushback.

Johanson is also the architect behind BIOCOM's deployment at Garganta. He pitched the system to Dean Lockwood as a superior alternative to human command officers -- a digital intelligence that "doesn't sleep, doesn't make bad decisions based on compassion, and has a 100% success rate." His confidence in BIOCOM's infallibility proved catastrophic, as the system was eventually subverted by the Warden, stripping all human control from the facility.

Prior to Garganta, Johanson's reputation was already controversial. The "Johanson Papers" -- leaked documents revealing that prolonged hydrostasis caused permanent memory loss in subjects -- prompted the European Space Agency to sever ties with Kovac in 2044, following the disastrous Red Alpha Mars mission. The scandal did nothing to slow Johanson's ambitions. He simply redirected KDS's hydrostasis technology from space exploration to military application, weaponizing the very side effect that had discredited it: memory-wiped subjects became compliant soldiers for the Legion program.

## Arc

- **2044**: The Johanson Papers leak exposes hydrostasis memory loss. ESA cuts ties with Kovac after the Red Alpha Mars mission disaster.
- **July 2050**: Johanson contacts Janson Davies, pitching KDS services to the Garganta operation by demonstrating how easily its secrets could be discovered.
- **September 2051**: Pressures Harriet Lorde over KDS-Deep excavation delays, demanding construction begin immediately and setting an aggressive move-in deadline of January 2053.
- **2051-2052**: Champions BIOCOM deployment, pitching it to Dean Lockwood as the solution for all facility operations, security, and resource management.
- **August 2053**: Instructs Piros to suppress information about a Santonian employee found in the hydrostasis storage area, directing him to take "no further action" unless directly confronted.
- **Post-2053**: Largely absent from the log record. His influence persists through the systems he installed -- BIOCOM, KDS-Deep, the Legion program -- all of which outlast human oversight of the facility.

## Key Relationships

- **Andre Piros (A134)** -- Johanson's operational right hand at Garganta. Piros carries out directives, handles security matters, and serves as the buffer between Johanson and facility staff.
- **Janson Davies (A001)** -- Dreyfus Industries executive whom Johanson recruited as a client. Their relationship is transactional: Johanson provides security and secrecy; Davies provides funding and access.
- **Dean Lockwood (A074)** -- Facility authority to whom Johanson pitched BIOCOM. Lockwood served as the local decision-maker that Johanson needed to win over for KDS expansion.
- **Harriet Lorde (B273)** -- Construction coordinator whom Johanson pressured over KDS-Deep excavation scheduling.
- **Ellis Carnegie** -- Kovac Research cybernetics specialist who reported to Johanson on operative behavioral encoding and the transition to Rise-manufactured soldiers.
