## Background

Boris Stanovich is a report analyst in the Medical Department at the Garganta Complex. His role places him at the intersection of raw data and institutional failure: he compiles health statistics, incident reports, and lockdown procedures while the systems he documents systematically refuse to act on his findings. He is the voice of institutional sanity in a collapsing system -- measured, data-driven, and increasingly urgent in his recommendations, all of which go unheeded.

Stanovich's reports are models of professional restraint. He presents alarming findings with clinical detachment, letting the numbers carry the weight. When he documents a 34.4% psychological disturbance rate across the entire SMC workforce, he does not editorialize. When he notes that 8,350 man-hours have been lost to malaise, lethargy, and exhaustion, he simply recommends shifting from a 3-shift to a 4-shift workforce to allow mandatory sunlight exposure. His recommendations are practical, achievable, and almost certainly ignored.

What makes Stanovich distinctive is the tension between the professional format he maintains and the horror he describes. He writes formal analyst reports with numbered headers, statistical breakdowns, and signed conclusions while documenting a workplace where a third of the employees are psychologically disturbed, creatures are being denied to exist by security forces, and over a hundred incidents go unreported because Kovac Defense Services has decided the Medical Department does not need to know what is happening in the mines.

## Arc

**December 2052 -- "Garganta Flu" report.** Stanovich files report #SAMD-C043-07, documenting an alarming spike in the suicide rate during the second half of 2052. He identifies a steady rise in psycho-pharmaceutical use beginning in April 2052, with 34.4% of the SMC workforce reporting psychological disturbances. Physical health has also declined: reports of malaise, lethargy, and "wandering" employees, particularly among lower-level construction teams. He coins or records the term "Garganta Flu" for the influenza-like condition with no identifiable physical cause. His recommendation -- sunlight exposure and exercise -- is humane, practical, and entirely inadequate for what is actually happening (early NAM-V infection and proximity to alien biology).

**September 2053 -- Incident suppression report.** Stanovich files report #SAMD-C047-15, comparing Medical Center admissions data with official incident reports. He discovers that at least 114 incidents within BIOCOM-controlled areas have gone unreported by KDS. These range from burns and broken bones to psychosis, hallucinations, and suicide attempts. Patients arrive at the Medical Center without context because KDS refuses to file incident reports. When Stanovich's department demands transparency, Anders Johanson (KDS) responds: "Your job is to treat the wounded, not to analyze the battlefield. Patch them up and send them back." Stanovich notes that KDS is actively denying the existence of creatures that medical staff have directly encountered. His report concludes with the dry observation: "We also need to order more supplies."

**October 2053 -- Lockdown procedures.** Following the catastrophic energy surge that kills Andrew Clinton and devastates parts of the complex, Stanovich is tasked with compiling facility-wide lockdown procedures. His report (#SAMD-C066-32) is a comprehensive document covering medical, engineering, environment control, communications, and security protocols. He notes with characteristic honesty where his information is incomplete: multiple departments did not respond to his requests, and several KDS and Project Insight protocols are listed with the parenthetical "(I have no further details on this protocol - C066)." The document reveals the extreme measures being taken -- welding shut reactor containment doors, engaging Faraday locks, deactivating cell grids, and sealing all external communications to tier 5 only.

**After October 2053 -- Unknown.** No further reports from Stanovich appear in the log archive. Given the escalating crisis and the fact that the complex was ultimately abandoned and overrun by 2055-2056, his fate is uncertain.

## Key Relationships

- **Anders Johanson (A029)** -- KDS leader who stonewalls Stanovich's requests for information. Johanson's dismissive response ("Patch them up and send them back") exemplifies the institutional contempt that Stanovich documents without comment.
- **Medical Department staff** -- Stanovich represents their collective frustration. His reports channel the voices of doctors and medics who cannot do their jobs because security forces are rewriting facts.
- **KDS operatives** -- Patients who arrive at the trauma center mute, either unable or forbidden to explain what happened to them in the BIOCOM-controlled exclusion zone.
- **Eric Chapman (C368)** -- Medical records clerk who shares observations of unexplained noises with Stanovich, referenced in other logs.
