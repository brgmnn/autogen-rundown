## Background

Dr. Abeo Dauda was a Nigerian scientist working within Garganta as a virus researcher, likely under the umbrella of Project Insight. His civilian employee ID was A153, placing him at the executive/senior specialist tier. Dauda conducted extensive experiments on NAM-V, producing research that James Durant cited as foundational -- "Dr. Dauda's theory that the virus happened upon the parasite at some point, found it to be an appropriate host, and evolved to manipulate the behavior of the parasite to ensure transmission." Durant also noted that "Dr. Dauda's research shows such resilience in the virus."

Dauda's own research report to Dean Lockwood (A074) reveals a clinical, methodical mind and a disturbing level of admiration for his subject. He documented NAM-V's multipronged attack strategy: transmission through any bodily fluid (sweat, blood, saliva, bile, urine, even aqueous and vitreous humors), survival for weeks airborne or on surfaces, and an 85% infection rate across all exposure methods. The remaining 15% who resisted other exposure methods contracted the virus when exposed to vaporous environments heavily dosed with infected fomites. His conclusion: "The virus is remarkable. It appears to have been perfectly designed to infect regardless of the circumstance."

Notably, Dauda received live test subjects from Andre Piros (KDS operational commander) for his experiments, and was planning vacuum exposure tests pending delivery of "new subjects." This places him squarely within the moral machinery of Garganta -- a scientist whose research required human experimentation facilitated by KDS.

When the facility collapsed and creatures overran the Complex, Dauda's civilian status offered no protection. He followed a group of KSO operatives to KDS-Deep, where he witnessed the systematic HSU evacuation: mechanical arms delivering empty hydrostasis units, guards climbing in, arms carrying them away into the darkness. When he revealed himself, Hackett nearly shot him on sight. Only Woods's intervention -- "Exfil, soldier" -- saved his life. Woods directed Dauda into an HSU with the blunt choice: "Get in... Or you can die, brother." Dauda complied, and was assigned the KSO designation T-3701.

## Arc

**Virus Research (pre-2054):** Active researcher within Garganta, studying NAM-V. Develops foundational theories about the virus-parasite symbiotic relationship. Conducts controlled exposure experiments with human subjects provided by KDS. Produces research cited by Durant and referenced by other scientists.

**Research Findings:** Documents NAM-V's extraordinary transmission capabilities and characterizes it as "perfectly designed." His work confirms the virus is an "omnipotent force of nature" that evolves with every host, creating potentially millions of strains. Identifies hydrostasis as the only method of killing the virus within a host.

**The Fall of Garganta (~2055-2056):** As the Complex is overrun, Dauda finds himself without the protection that KDS or military status afforded others. He follows KSO operatives to KDS-Deep, narrating his experience in real-time: "I must talk to them. Come on, Abeo, you can do this." His desperation is palpable -- the scientist who once requisitioned human subjects is now begging soldiers not to shoot him.

**Conversion to KSO:** Enters an HSU at KDS-Deep, assigned tactical designation T-3701. His dual identity as a former A-tier scientist and a KSO prisoner is unique among the four player characters. All the knowledge he accumulated about NAM-V, the parasites, and the creatures is locked behind hydrostasis-induced memory loss.

**Quad B452 (~2063):** Assigned by the Warden/BIOCOM system alongside Bishop, Woods, and Hackett. Status: Seek and Destroy. The man who once studied the virus now fights through the creatures it created, with no memory of the research that could have informed his survival.

## Key Relationships

- **Dr. James Durant (B255)** -- Colleague. Durant worked under Dauda's theoretical framework regarding the virus-parasite relationship and cited his research extensively.
- **Dean Lockwood (A074)** -- Received Dauda's virus research reports. Dauda reported directly to senior facility authority.
- **Andre Piros (A134)** -- Supplied Dauda with human test subjects for NAM-V exposure experiments.
- **Woods (F-2056)** -- Saved Dauda's life at KDS-Deep. The only reason Dauda survived to become a KSO.
- **Hackett (O-4711)** -- Nearly killed Dauda at KDS-Deep before Woods intervened.
- **Bishop (GO-1395)** -- Fellow Quad B452 member.
