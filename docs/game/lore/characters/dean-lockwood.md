## Background

Dean Lockwood holds tier 5 clearance -- the highest security level at Garganta -- and operates as the facility's senior administrative authority. His employee ID prefix "A" marks him as an executive-tier figure, and throughout the logs he functions as the point of contact between Santonian operations, Kovac Defense Services, and the broader corporate hierarchy. Every major security concern, terminology directive, and information containment order passes through his desk.

Lockwood's defining characteristic is his obsession with narrative control. When workers began calling the NAM-V virus by colloquial names -- "the Garganta Flu," "the Stoops," "Stoop Throat," "the Pits" -- Lockwood moved swiftly to ban the terms. His directive to Andrew Clinton was unambiguous: connecting the virus to the facility could trigger a public relations disaster, and all employees must be trained on the correct nomenclature. His team would "continue to work on decoding the virus," he wrote, while Clinton's team must "start controlling the narrative." The phrasing reveals a man who sees information management as equal in priority to the scientific response.

Lockwood was also the first facility authority to raise alarms about Kovac's inadequate security presence. In a heated email to Andre Piros, he catalogued security failures: fishing vessels breaching the restricted zone, civilians wandering near the facility, violent incidents among staff, and deep excavation sites left unguarded despite Johanson's promises. He wrote directly to Piros demanding a meeting, invoking assurances from "Mr. Dreyfus himself" that Kovac would handle security. This letter reveals a Lockwood who, at least in the early period, was willing to push back against KDS when operational reality diverged from corporate promises.

After Andrew Clinton's death in the October 2053 energy surge, Lockwood assumed broader project management responsibilities. He had already been the one approving or blocking new projects -- it was Lockwood who designated the area for Project Insight, holding up KDS construction until those plans were finalized. In the post-Clinton era, Lockwood became increasingly isolated as the decision-maker for a facility spiraling out of control, navigating the competing demands of KDS operations, Project Insight research, and a workforce being ravaged by a virus whose very name he had tried to control.

## Arc

- **2050**: Lockwood is established as a senior authority at Garganta, holding up KDS-Deep construction until Project Insight plans are finalized. Complains to Andre Piros about inadequate Kovac security presence.
- **July 2052**: Receives report from Piros about an unknown signal being broadcast from within the facility. Responds curtly: "What do you mean a 'signal'? Whatever it is, it is not authorized."
- **2052**: Issues directive to Andrew Clinton banning colloquial terms for NAM-V. Demands Santonian employees use only the official designation. Instructs Clinton to "start controlling the narrative."
- **2053**: After Clinton's death in the energy surge, Lockwood assumes expanded project management role. Becomes the primary administrative authority coordinating between departments.
- **April 2054**: Encounters Rise Project shipments and demands explanation from Piros, who deflects: "If you haven't been informed, I assume you don't need to know." Lockwood is shut out of KDS's classified programs despite his tier 5 clearance.
- **2054+**: Increasingly isolated as the facility's sole remaining high-clearance administrator, grappling with security breakdown and viral catastrophe while the corporate structure crumbles around him.

## Key Relationships

- **Andre Piros (A134)** -- KDS operational commander at Garganta. Lockwood's relationship with Piros is contentious; he demands security improvements from KDS while Piros deflects questions about classified programs and asserts he answers to his own chain of command.
- **Andrew Clinton (B035)** -- SMC project manager who received Lockwood's NAM-V terminology directive. Clinton was Lockwood's primary interface with the mining workforce. His death in the 2053 energy surge left Lockwood to absorb his responsibilities.
- **Anders Johanson (A029)** -- KDS leader who pitched BIOCOM to Lockwood. Johanson needed Lockwood's approval to expand KDS operations, making Lockwood the gatekeeper for Kovac's ambitions at the facility.
- **Janson Davies (A001)** -- Dreyfus Industries executive. Lockwood references Davies's agreement on banning virus terminology, indicating a direct line to the corporate parent.
- **Harriet Lorde (B273)** -- Construction coordinator who answered to Lockwood rather than KDS, as she pointedly reminded Johanson.
