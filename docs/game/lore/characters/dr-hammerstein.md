## Background

Dr. Gallus Hammerstein was the founding lead researcher of Project Insight, the division responsible for studying the alien artifacts and creatures discovered deep within the Garganta facility. A geologist by training, Hammerstein positioned himself as the authority on all biological and archaeological discoveries within the Complex. He was funded generously by Dreyfus Industries and used those resources to build what he called "The Hammerstein Collection" -- a growing museum of preserved creature specimens intended for eventual public display, complete with photographic records for "the inevitable book and tour lectures."

Hammerstein's defining characteristic was his towering ego. His personal vault notes read less like scientific documentation and more like the diary of a man building a monument to himself. He brainstormed book titles obsessively ("A Revised Origin of the Species," "The Hammerstein Principle," "The Gallus Theory -- The Evolutionary of God's Monsters"). He named the entire genus of Garganta creatures "Gallus" after his own first name and insisted his staff temporarily call the parasites "Parasitidae Hammerstein." His Latin motto was "Non ducor, duco" -- "I am not led; I lead."

Beneath the grandiosity, Hammerstein was a genuinely capable researcher. His creature taxonomy identified four distinct species -- Gallus Reptus, Jaculus, Capreolatus, and Horridus -- and he made the critical observation that these creatures shared no common ancestors. "They are not branches; they are trees." This led him to the revolutionary conclusion that the creatures originated from multiple different worlds, a finding that would later be confirmed by the discovery of the Collectors and their multiverse-spanning mission. He also noted that darker-pigmented variants were more active hunters, and that mutations intensified at greater depths, suggesting an environmental driver.

However, Hammerstein was dismissive of colleagues who challenged him, particularly Dr. Rebecca Stokes, who presented evidence that some creatures contained human DNA. He rejected her RNA editing hypothesis with disdain, refused to consider that creatures might be mutated Garganta staff, and generally treated his team as threats to be managed rather than collaborators. His personal notes include the chilling directive: "Remove those clearly attempting to undermine me and upset the balance of power... Next week, the culling begins."

## Arc

**Founding Period (~2051-2052):** Establishes Project Insight with Dreyfus backing. Begins cataloging artifacts and specimens. Conducts early geological analysis. Reports the first unbreakable artifact (L18-AT06), concluding the material "does not appear to be from Earth."

**Taxonomy and Ego (2053-2054):** Develops the Gallus genus classification system. Records weekly notes in a private markdown vault to prevent colleagues from stealing his research. Brainstorms book titles. Plans "the culling" of rivals. Notes persistent sickness -- "My head is heavy. My brain feels damp. At times I feel like something is living inside me" -- likely early NAM-V infection symptoms.

**The Frequency Experiment (October 12th, 2053):** Plans a behavioral analysis experiment projecting a range of frequencies through the lower levels of Garganta, including near the Inner, to study creature reactions to aural stimulation. Assures everyone it will be "perfectly safe if everyone does as I say." The experiment triggers a catastrophic energy surge from the Inner that creates blast tunnels throughout the Complex, kills multiple people (including Andrew Clinton), and fundamentally changes the power structure of Garganta.

**Death (June 12th, 2055):** Killed alongside Nolan Connor, Keiran Briggs, and Nadelle Gaubert while exploring the Intrepid Chamber at Shaft 17b, 4,020 meters deep. The team was attacked by creatures. Dr. Stokes was tasked with writing the death announcement.

## Key Relationships

- **Dr. Rebecca Stokes (B162)** -- His chief scientific rival and eventual successor as Project Insight lead. Stokes challenged his taxonomy with evidence of human DNA in creatures. Hammerstein dismissed her work with "disdain." After his death, Stokes admitted in a draft: "I am glad he's dead. I hated him, as did all of you."
- **Janson Davies (A001)** -- Dreyfus Industries executive who funded Project Insight. Davies forwarded Hammerstein's artifact report to Dean Lockwood with the note: "Hammerstein is a blowhard."
- **Dean Lockwood (A074)** -- Senior facility authority who received Hammerstein's reports through Davies. Part of the inner circle that managed Hammerstein's ambitions.
- **Nolan Connor (B229)** -- Project Insight colleague killed alongside Hammerstein. Stokes described Connor as someone she would "personally miss the most."
- **Nadelle Gaubert** -- Researcher whose quantum entanglement work Stokes acknowledged in her draft eulogy. Killed with Hammerstein.
