## Background

Dr. August Teale served as the primary psychotherapist at the Garganta facility, responsible for assessing and treating the mental health of the workforce. In a facility where one-third of workers reported psychological disturbances, where suicide rates were climbing to alarming levels, and where an alien virus was quietly rewriting the biological reality of everyone present, Teale occupied one of the most impossible positions in the Complex. He was the person people came to when things stopped making sense, and he was expected to make sense of it for them.

Teale's early sessions demonstrate a competent clinician -- calm, measured, professional. He used standard pharmacological approaches (Haloperidol, Thorazine, Clozapine) and maintained structured therapeutic frameworks. He asked the right questions, kept appropriate boundaries, and documented everything through the HearSay system. But the cases he encountered were not standard. His patients were not merely anxious or depressed -- they were experiencing something that defied clinical classification.

His patient caseload included Mia, a worker who believed a bug had burrowed into her scalp and who forced Teale to touch the wound while screaming "It's in all of us," and Edgar Soweta, whose visual and auditory hallucinations proved resistant to every medication available. Soweta progressed from passive visions to active psychosis, and Teale cycled through increasingly aggressive pharmaceutical interventions without effect. The Clozapine dosage reached 700mg per day -- near the maximum -- with minimal impact. These were not hallucinations that medication could reach, because they were not, in the strictest sense, hallucinations.

## Arc

**Professional Clinician (pre-2053):** Conducts structured therapy sessions. Maintains clinical distance. Documents cases through HearSay. Treats a growing number of patients with similar symptom profiles -- visual and auditory hallucinations, paranoia, irrational behavior.

**Mia Session (2052-2053):** Encounters Mia, who presents with severe scalp scratching and claims a bug burrowed into her skin. The session escalates rapidly: Mia grabs Teale's hand and forces it to her head, demanding he feel the creature. She becomes violent. Teale calls for Dr. Banks. Mia's final words before the session ends: "It's in all of us." This is Teale's first direct physical confrontation with the possibility that the "hallucinations" are real.

**Edgar Soweta Treatment (January-April 2053):** Treats Edgar Soweta (Employee E105) for treatment-resistant psychosis. Attempts Haloperidol, Thorazine, and Clozapine at escalating doses. Soweta refuses medication, citing cognitive fog. Teale notes 14 patients presenting with identical symptoms. During a session update, he is interrupted by a noise from the walls: "Can you stop that noise please. I'm trying to work here. I think there's something in the wall or..." He asks engineering to investigate. This is the first sign that Teale himself is beginning to hear what his patients hear.

**Soweta Medication Refusal (January 31st 2053):** Soweta refuses pills outright. Teale notes the hallucinations are causing distress but Soweta insists "They're not hallucinations." Teale corrects himself to "visions." He cannot force treatment and places Soweta on medical leave.

**Psychological Collapse (April 7th 2053):** Soweta is released from the Complex by KDS. Teale records a session update that spirals into stream-of-consciousness breakdown. He hears the noise again -- "It's like insects in my skull." He sees movement in the corners. He acknowledges his assistant Susan has been "ignoring" him and "looking at" him. He forgets that HearSay is still recording and speaks his private fears aloud: "What if they want to see how much it affects me? This thing, that's changing people, what if it's me they really want? What if... what if Edgar was put here to test me?" His final recorded words: "I have to get out of here."

## Key Relationships

- **Mia** -- Patient whose body horror session was a turning point. Her violent insistence that "It's in all of us" planted the seed of Teale's own doubt about what was real.
- **Edgar Soweta (E105)** -- Long-term patient with treatment-resistant psychosis. Soweta's refusal of medication and insistence that his visions were real gradually eroded Teale's clinical certainty. When KDS removed Soweta, Teale was left alone with the same symptoms.
- **Susan** -- Teale's assistant. In his final recording, Teale expresses distrust of her, believing she is watching him. He calls for her repeatedly but she does not appear.
- **Dr. Banks** -- Medical colleague Teale calls for help during the Mia session. Represents the institutional support system that is failing.
- **Boris Stanovich (C066)** -- Medical Department analyst who documented the same facility-wide psychological crisis Teale was treating from the clinical side. Stanovich reported 34.4% of the workforce with psychological disturbances.
