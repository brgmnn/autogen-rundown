## Background

Edgar Soweta was a mechanic at Garganta, assigned to mine operations. His employee classification (E-prefix) placed him among the lowest tier of the facility hierarchy -- manual laborers with minimal institutional power and maximal exposure to the hazards of the deep complex.

Soweta became a patient of Dr. August Teale after presenting with visual and auditory hallucinations, potential psychosis, and paranoia that intensified at night. His case became one of the most significant in Teale's practice -- not because Soweta was unusual, but because he was the most resistant to treatment and the most insistent that what he experienced was real.

## Arc

**The Patient (January 2053)**

Soweta's first documented session with Teale shows a man who has shut down. He speaks in fragments, nearly inaudible. He refuses his medication because it makes him "too foggy" -- an ironic choice of words given the literal fog that would later fill Garganta's corridors. When Teale characterizes his experiences as hallucinations, Soweta corrects him firmly: "They're not hallucinations." Teale backpedals to the word "visions," a concession that reveals the therapist's own uncertainty.

Teale threatens to put Soweta on medical leave if he refuses medication. Soweta accepts this without protest. He would rather not work than take the pills that dull whatever it is he perceives.

**Treatment-Resistant (February 2053)**

By February, Teale has cycled through Haloperidol, Thorazine, and Clozapine at 700mg/day with minimal effect. Soweta is one of 14 patients presenting identical symptoms. Teale's clinical notes on Soweta begin to break down -- he interrupts himself, hears noises in the walls, and loses his train of thought. The patient's condition is becoming the doctor's condition.

**Released by KDS (April 2053)**

In April 2053, Teale records that he "let him go." KDS removed Soweta from the complex. Teale doubts they will ever see him again. But the log's most disturbing content is not about Soweta's departure -- it is about what happened to Teale afterward. "The more I listened to him, the more sense it made," Teale admits. Soweta's insistence that his experiences were real -- that something was in the walls, that the noises were not hallucinations -- had begun to convince his own therapist.

Teale's final reflection on Soweta's case tips into full paranoia: "What if Edgar was put here to test me?"

## Key Relationships

- **Dr. August Teale (B023):** Treating psychotherapist. Soweta's case was pivotal in Teale's own psychological deterioration. Teale went from diagnosing Soweta to experiencing the same symptoms.
- **KDS:** Removed Soweta from the complex. The circumstances and destination are unspecified.
- **Other Patients:** Soweta was one of at least 14 patients presenting identical symptoms of hallucinations and paranoia, suggesting a systemic cause rather than individual pathology.
