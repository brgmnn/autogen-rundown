## Background

Eric Chapman was a clerk in the medical records department at Garganta. Self-identified as autistic, Chapman possessed an unusually direct communication style and an observational acuity that most of his colleagues lacked or had learned to suppress. His role processing medical records gave him access to personnel files and psychiatric evaluations -- a vantage point from which the facility's deterioration was quantifiable rather than merely felt.

Chapman had processed 481 confirmed cases of NAM-V by the time he wrote his only known communication, making him one of the few people at Garganta who had a concrete, data-driven understanding of the outbreak's scope. His co-workers spoke to him but, by his own assessment, did not value his perspective. This social isolation, combined with his analytical nature, led him to seek connection through the most logical channel available: a carefully reasoned letter of introduction.

## Arc

Chapman's single known log is a letter to Boris Stanovich, the Medical Department's report analyst. The letter is remarkable for its transparency. Chapman lays out his qualifications for friendship with the methodical precision of a compatibility assessment: shared professional interests, complementary personality traits, observed behavioral patterns. He notes that Stanovich is tidy, clean, an analyst, fond of numbers, often alone, always eats dessert, and once smiled at him in the cafeteria.

But buried within this earnest social overture is a detail of genuine significance. Chapman reveals that he read Stanovich's psychiatric evaluation, in which Stanovich reported hearing unexplained noises from the walls while working in the mass spectrometry laboratory. Chapman then went to the same laboratory and heard the same noises. Rather than reporting them -- understanding, perhaps, that reports disappeared at Garganta -- he chose to raise the matter privately, as the foundation of a new friendship.

This detail connects Chapman to the broader pattern of auditory phenomena that plagued Garganta workers. Dr. Teale documented similar experiences in his patients and eventually in himself. The noises were an early manifestation of something far worse than anyone understood at the time.

## Key Relationships

- **Boris Stanovich (C066):** Target of Chapman's friendship initiative. Stanovich had reported hearing unexplained noises -- a detail Chapman independently verified.
- **Dr. August Teale (B023):** Conducted Stanovich's psychiatric evaluation, which Chapman read as part of his medical records access. Teale himself later reported hearing similar noises.
- **Medical Records Co-workers:** Chapman noted they spoke to him but did not value his perspective.
