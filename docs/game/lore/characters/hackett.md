## Background

Aiden Hackett grew up on the island of Colt, off the coast of Skerries, Ireland, at the family estate of Braeburn Manor -- the last remaining house on an island locals said was cursed. The "Mallacht of Colt" (Curse of Colt) was blamed on an outcast druidic sect driven from the island in the eighteenth century, and the island had a centuries-long history of misfortune, unexplained deaths, and tragedy.

The curse found the Hackett family twice. At five years old, Aiden watched his mother fall to her death from the sea cliffs. Whether she fell or jumped was never determined, but the trauma was profound. Seven years later, at age twelve, Aiden was out hunting rabbits with his fourteen-year-old brother Cormac when the gun he was carrying discharged, striking Cormac in the neck. With no emergency services on the island, Cormac bled out before help arrived. The police investigation remained open, with "many unanswered questions." Aiden had brothers Rory and Killian as well, and a father who remained on the island.

How Hackett came to be in the Legion program is unique among the four playable characters: he joined voluntarily. While the specific circumstances of his enlistment are not detailed in the logs, his willingness stands in contrast to Bishop and Woods (both prisoners sent by force) and Dauda (a civilian scientist who entered HSU to survive). During his six-month evaluation, Hackett demonstrated the aggressive, profane demeanor that defines him -- but also a sharp awareness of his situation. He remembered his own name immediately and challenged his evaluator's authority at every turn.

Despite the hydrostasis conditioning, Hackett could not recall his family when prompted with their names. The memories of Cormac, Rory, Killian, and his father produced no emotional response. Whether this erasure was a mercy or a violation is left ambiguous.

## Arc

**Childhood on Colt (2020s-2030s):** Grows up under the shadow of the island's curse. Witnesses his mother's death at age five. Accidentally kills his brother Cormac at age twelve. The trauma shapes him but the details are eventually erased by hydrostasis.

**Voluntary Enlistment:** At some point, Hackett joins the Legion program willingly -- the only one of the four player characters to do so. His motivations are not explicitly stated in the logs, but a man who carries the weight of his mother's death and his brother's blood on his hands may have had reason to seek oblivion.

**Six-Month Evaluation:** Awakened from hydrostasis. Immediately combative and profane. Remembers his own name ("Aiden Hackett. That's my name.") but has no memory of his family when their names are read to him. Subjected to NGF protein clusters, serotonin, and Semantic-02 programming for further conditioning.

**KDS-Deep Evacuation:** Present at KDS-Deep as operatives retreat into HSUs. Nearly shoots Dr. Dauda on sight ("Your lucky day") before Woods intervenes. Complies with Woods's order despite being the more aggressive of the two.

**Quad B452 (~2063):** Assigned by the Warden/BIOCOM system alongside Bishop, Woods, and Dauda. Status: Seek and Destroy. Deployed on expeditions into the Complex.

## Key Relationships

- **Cormac Hackett** -- Brother, killed by Aiden's hand at age fourteen. The memory is erased by hydrostasis but the event defines Hackett's pre-Legion identity.
- **Woods (F-2056)** -- Fellow Quad B452 member. Woods overrides Hackett during the KDS-Deep evacuation, preventing him from killing Dauda. Hackett complies, suggesting a degree of respect for or deference to Woods despite his rebellious nature.
- **Dauda (T-3701)** -- Almost killed Dauda at KDS-Deep. Their relationship begins with Hackett's gun in Dauda's face and Woods's intervention.
- **Bishop (GO-1395)** -- Fellow Quad B452 member.
- **KDS Handlers** -- Hackett's evaluators find him interesting but difficult. He retains his name and personality through conditioning more readily than expected.
