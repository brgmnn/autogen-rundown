## Background

Howard Stokes was a geologist employed by SMC who worked primarily off-site, providing spectroscopy analysis and material science expertise to the Garganta operation. He is related to Dr. Rebecca Stokes, the Project Insight researcher who took over as lead analyst after Hammerstein's death and eventually oversaw the EF-01 expedition. The exact nature of their family relationship (siblings, cousins, or otherwise) is not explicitly stated, but Rebecca refers to him informally as "Howie" in her audio logs.

Howard occupied a unique position in the Garganta ecosystem: close enough to receive artifact samples and provide critical scientific analysis, but far enough removed from the facility to maintain a collaborative, measured scientific perspective unburdened by the paranoia and desperation that consumed those working underground. His spectroscopy work on samples from the Inner provided some of the earliest hard evidence that the materials found in Garganta were not of terrestrial origin.

## Arc

Howard Stokes' earliest known contribution is his March 2052 spectroscopy report on artifact sample L18-AT07-A004-UBK, sent to Dr. Hammerstein. His analysis confirmed that the material was "truly unique" -- an unknown alloy comprised of almost 24% unidentified material. He proposed a collaborative approach: his off-site lab would attempt to replicate the alloy from known components while Hammerstein's team searched for the unknown materials locally.

His hypothesis that the unknown materials were "of extra-terrestrial origin dispersed during the Chicxulub impact event" proved remarkably prescient. The Chicxulub crater was not formed by a natural asteroid but by the Collectors' spacecraft -- the Inner itself -- making any materials dispersed during that impact genuinely alien in origin.

Later, Rebecca Stokes referenced sending samples to Howard for analysis of the Matter Wave Projector, indicating his off-site lab remained a trusted resource for the most sensitive material analysis throughout the project's life.
