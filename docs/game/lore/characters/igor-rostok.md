## Background

Igor Rostok was a generalist engineer at Garganta, one of the people who physically built and maintained the underground complex. He was a "company man through and through," as Andrew Clinton described him -- loyal, capable, and deeply invested in the facility's operations. Igor had personal knowledge of Garganta's infrastructure that few others possessed, including detailed spatial data on nearly every section of the complex.

Igor was also a man with a defiant sense of humor and a growing awareness that something was profoundly wrong with how Garganta was being run. He watched KDS security tighten from reasonable precaution into outright oppression, and he was not the type to stay quiet about it.

## Arc

**The Engineer (2050-2052)**

Igor's earliest known contribution is a detailed spatial audit of Garganta's facilities, sent to fellow engineer Phil Decker. The document reveals Igor's intimate knowledge of the complex: over 1.8 million square meters of space across mining, research, habitation, and military zones, with 185,000 square meters of unregistered space and entire levels of excavation that KDS had "completely taken over" with no records available. His sign-off -- "I'll fill in the blanks if I get KDS clearance. Or maybe I'll just walk in like I own the place ;/" -- shows both his curiosity and his willingness to push boundaries. That casual defiance would cost him everything.

**The Dissident (2053)**

By mid-2053, Igor was openly furious. The HearSay surveillance system had been installed, social meetings were empty because workers were terrified of speaking to each other, and KDS "thugs" enforced order through intimidation. Igor wrote to Andrew Clinton -- his friend, though their relationship had grown strained as Clinton's managerial responsibilities consumed him -- pleading for action. He drew explicit parallels to historical authoritarianism ("My grandfather told me about this shit when he was a boy"), called out Project Insight's elitism, and demanded a workers' charter.

**The Martyr (July 28, 2053)**

Igor was shot and killed by a KSO for "entering a restricted area." That was the full extent of the information KDS provided. Andre Piros, KDS operational commander, defended the killing by citing the employee contract's provisions for "punitive consequence" under martial law. Janson Davies, the Dreyfus Industries executive, expressed sympathy while carefully maintaining plausible deniability.

Igor's death was the catalyst for Andrew Clinton's decision to go to the surface and expose Garganta to the outside world. Clinton was at the helipad, ready to leave, when the October 13th energy surge from the Inner killed him -- an event the lore strongly implies was directed by the Collectors specifically to prevent exposure of the facility.

## Key Relationships

- **Andrew Clinton (B035):** Friend and advocate. Clinton was devastated by Igor's death and tried to escalate the matter to Dreyfus Industries. Igor's murder ultimately motivated Clinton's attempt to expose Garganta.
- **Phil Decker (C085):** Fellow engineer. Igor shared detailed facility spatial data with Decker.
- **Andre Piros (A134):** KDS commander who defended Igor's execution by citing contractual provisions for martial law enforcement.
- **Janson Davies (A001):** Dreyfus executive who expressed hollow condolences while deflecting responsibility.
