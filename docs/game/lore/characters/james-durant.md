## Background

James Durant is a scientist attached to Project Insight at the Garganta Complex, specializing in virology and the relationship between the NAM-V virus and its parasitic hosts. Operating under the theoretical framework established by Dr. Abeo Dauda, Durant conducted the definitive analysis of NAM-V's extraterrestrial origin, demonstrating that both the virus and the parasites (Parasitidae Garganta) evolved under similar environmental conditions and almost certainly share a common origin beyond Earth.

Durant's research was meticulous and consequential. He established the symbiotic mechanism between virus and parasite: NAM-V triggers life-prolonging chemicals in the parasite, while the parasite provides a suspendable organic structure for the virus and ensures transmission to new hosts. He discovered that each infected parasite carries its own viral mutation, meaning any host with multiple parasites becomes a gene-shuffling factory for new strains -- potentially millions of them. This finding explained both the physical deformities seen in infected organisms and the near-impossibility of developing a cure.

Beyond his scientific work, Durant was a man capable of warmth and deep connection. His correspondence with Dr. Susan Nesbitt reveals a romantic relationship conducted through encrypted emails, mixing professional excitement with genuine affection. He signed his letters "xxx" and pleaded with her to join the EF-01 expedition so he would not have to experience the discovery without her. She declined, absorbed in her own breakthrough with protein sequencing of Gramineae plants.

## Arc

**~2054 -- Virus research at Garganta.** Durant works under the supervision of Dean Lockwood (A074), reporting directly on NAM-V findings. His June 2054 email to Lockwood is the definitive scientific analysis of NAM-V: extraterrestrial origin confirmed, symbiotic mechanism mapped, and a grim prognosis. He identifies hydrostasis as the only method of killing the virus, but notes it is merely a temporary measure since reinfection occurs immediately after resuscitation. He concludes that the only hope lies in finding an "immune genome" at the virus's point of origin: "Evolution has had millions of years to fight the virus. We have months."

**~2054-2055 -- Romance with Susan Nesbitt.** Durant's emails to Nesbitt show a different side: excited about pioneer discovery, bored in pre-mission isolation, and genuinely affectionate. He urges her to join EF-01, but she is consumed by her protein sequencer breakthrough and cannot abandon four years of work. She sends him off with encouragement: "You're a good friend James. Get home safely."

**~2055 -- EF-01 expedition.** Durant leads the first human team through the Matter Wave Projector to another dimension. The team, which includes KSO Bishop among its security detail, arrives in a jungle environment teeming with vegetation and water. The link to Garganta is immediately severed -- no comms, no telemetry, no quantum tether. Durant orders worst-case protocols: permanent camp, defensive perimeter, and long-term survival preparations. He is confident they will find food and water.

**Years later -- Loss of Bishop.** After years stranded on the alien world, the datacubes -- dormant since arrival -- suddenly show activity. Alice, a team member, detects a quantum frequency reading and claims to hear Nolan Connor's voice calling for EF-01. She directs Bishop to check the datacubes. Upon touching them, Bishop vanishes -- teleported back to Garganta, where he is immediately taken away by KDS operative Piros. Durant is devastated by the loss, describing Bishop as the one who convinced the other operatives to help build their new facility and outposts. He orders everyone to stay away from the datacubes, unable to risk losing anyone else.

**Endgame.** At the conclusion of Rundown 8, when the prisoners teleport to a clean dimension, they arrive at the very world where Durant's team has been surviving for years. Durant's voice is the last thing the players hear: he warns them not to move because there are traps everywhere.

## Key Relationships

- **Dr. Susan Nesbitt (B081)** -- Romantic partner. They exchange affectionate encrypted emails mixing scientific discussion with personal warmth. She declines to join EF-01, focused on her own Gramineae research.
- **Dean Lockwood (A074)** -- His tier-5 clearance supervisor. Durant reports virus research findings directly to Lockwood.
- **Dr. Abeo Dauda** -- Theoretical mentor. Durant works under Dauda's theory of virus-parasite origin. Dauda is also one of the four playable characters.
- **KSO Bishop** -- Security detail on EF-01, later a pillar of the stranded community. Bishop's disappearance via datacube is a devastating blow. Bishop is also one of the four playable characters.
- **Dr. Rebecca Stokes (B162)** -- Project Insight colleague. Nolan Connor sends Durant's recovered recordings to Stokes for analysis.
- **Nolan Connor (B229)** -- Project Insight comms/telemetry. Connor recovers Durant's fragmented recordings from the Matter Wave Projector memory bank.
- **Alice** -- EF-01 team member. Monitors quantum frequencies daily and detects the signal that leads to Bishop's return to Garganta.
