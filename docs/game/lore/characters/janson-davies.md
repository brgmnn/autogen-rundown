## Background

Janson Davies holds employee ID A001 -- the lowest number in the entire Garganta registry -- placing him at the apex of the corporate hierarchy. As a senior executive at Dreyfus Industries, the parent corporation of Santonian Mining Company, Davies occupies a carefully constructed position: close enough to direct the course of events at Garganta, distant enough to deny involvement when things go wrong. This duality defines every communication he produces.

Davies's primary rhetorical strategy is the disclaimer. In his most detailed surviving correspondence, he insists that "Dreyfus Industries does not get involved with the internal operations of our subsidiary organizations," that his previous communications were "purely inquisitiveness" on his part, and that he has "never dictated policy or direction to the Santonian Mining Company." In the same letter, he describes how he recommended key personnel for Project Insight, supported the SMC board's decision to launch it, and dictated the terms under which Andrew Clinton would continue mining operations -- now subject to Project Insight's exclusive authority over "where you drill, when you drill, what you drill, how you drill, and if you drill." The gap between his stated non-involvement and his actual influence is the defining feature of his character.

Davies was the target of Anders Johanson's initial KDS pitch in July 2050, when Johanson demonstrated how easily Garganta's secrets could be discovered -- the facility name, mass graves, concealed costs, unreported pathogens. Rather than viewing this as a threat, Davies accepted KDS as a partner, bringing Kovac on board to provide both physical security and data protection. This decision placed a private military contractor with martial law powers inside a civilian mining operation, a partnership that would eventually lead to worker executions, weaponized hydrostasis, and total corporate impunity.

When Andrew Clinton brought the execution of Igor Rostok to Davies's attention, begging for help against Kovac's overreach, Davies forwarded the complaint to Andre Piros -- the very person Clinton was complaining about. His cover note to Clinton expressed sympathy for the loss while noting he could "only say that I condone the use of excessive force in any circumstance" (likely an autocorrect or intentional typo for "condemn"), and emphasized that he was "merely an advisor to Santonian Industries" with "nothing to do with company policy or procedure." A man is dead, an employee is pleading for intervention, and Davies's response is to redirect the complaint back into the system that produced it.

## Arc

- **July 2050**: Receives Anders Johanson's pitch for KDS services, which lists Garganta's exposed secrets. Agrees to use D-Lock encrypted communications and brings KDS on board.
- **March 2052**: Writes to Andrew Clinton establishing communication protocols via D-Lock. Outlines the launch of Project Insight, disclaiming direct involvement while specifying its operational scope. Sends personal regards to Clinton's daughter Ariana on her birthday.
- **2050-2052**: Referenced in EBDT fragments as the recipient of progress updates from Clinton. Fragment 0BE201 shows Clinton reporting on construction progress; Fragment 0BE202 shows Clinton managing "Problem #12" and assuring Davies the matter is under control.
- **August 2053**: Receives Clinton's desperate plea about Igor Rostok's execution. Forwards the complaint to Andre Piros with a note distancing himself from any policy responsibility, then steps back entirely.
- **Post-2053**: Absent from the log record. Whether Davies survived the facility's collapse or evacuated to the surface is unknown.

## Key Relationships

- **Andrew Clinton (B035)** -- SMC project manager who reported to Davies on construction progress and later pleaded for help against KDS overreach. Davies's responses to Clinton mix personal warmth (birthday wishes for his daughter) with institutional deflection.
- **Anders Johanson (A029)** -- KDS leader who recruited Davies as a client through a demonstration of leverage. Their relationship is the foundational corporate partnership enabling everything that happens at Garganta.
- **Andre Piros (A134)** -- KDS operational commander to whom Davies redirected Clinton's complaint about the Rostok execution, effectively endorsing the chain of command that produced the killing.
- **Gustavo Peres** -- A figure Johanson identified as having "pressed for additional funds" and presenting "a liability." Peres appears to be a local governmental or administrative contact used by Dreyfus for the Yucatan operation.
- **Mr. Dreyfus** -- The head of Dreyfus Industries, referenced but never appearing directly. Lockwood notes that "Mr. Dreyfus himself" assured him Kovac would handle security.
