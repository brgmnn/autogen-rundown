## Background

John Schaeffer was an engineer employed by the Santonian Mining Company at the Garganta facility. He was one of two witnesses (alongside C085) to the disappearance of two mining engineers who first discovered and made physical contact with the Matter Wave Projector artifact. His personnel file was subsequently flagged with a note that Kovac was "dealing with" him, strongly implying he was forced into the Legion program and placed in hydrostasis against his will.

Like all subjects of the Legion program, extended hydrostasis exposure eroded Schaeffer's memories and identity. He was fitted with a Sec-B-M neural implant and deployed as a KSO operative under the Warden's control, designated T-3894. During one expedition, his entire squad was killed by creatures. Schaeffer survived and escaped, but instead of returning to his hydrostasis unit as the Warden demanded, he fled into the ventilation system and began fighting the implant's compulsions. His first recording captures the raw agony of this struggle -- a man torn between a voice screaming "EXTRACT! RETREAT! COMPLY!" and the fragments of identity clawing their way back.

Schaeffer successfully removed the neural implant from his brain stem through self-surgery, an act of extraordinary desperation that caused bleeding and possible infection but freed his thoughts. Over subsequent days his memory returned in fragments. He recalled his name, his profession, and an instinctive familiarity with the facility. He hacked into the network through a powered terminal, created credentials, and began systematically investigating his situation -- discovering the Warden program, the BIOCOM hijacking, and the true nature of KDS-Deep.

When he finally reached KDS-Deep, the horror of what he found crystallized his resolve. Thousands of people locked in hydrostasis coffins, their memories consumed as resources by an AI. He discovered his own message from seven years earlier -- a note left for rescuers who never came. He had voluntarily entered hydrostasis during the creature overrun, knowing it would destroy his mind. That act of desperate self-preservation cost him seven years and everything he was. The person who climbed into that unit died, but Schaeffer refused to accept that as the end.

## Arc

**Pre-Hydrostasis (pre-2050s):** SMC engineer at Garganta. Witnesses the Matter Wave Projector incident. Flagged by Kovac and likely forced into the Legion program.

**KSO Deployment (~2063):** Deployed as T-3894 in a four-person squad. Squad killed by creatures. Schaeffer escapes but cannot shake the Warden's voice commanding compliance.

**Awakening (R3):** Makes first recording in a state of confusion and terror. Cannot distinguish whether he is a prisoner or employee. Fights the implant's demands. Removes the device from his brain stem through self-surgery. Begins exploring the facility and observing other implant-controlled operatives, but cannot approach them safely.

**Investigation (R4):** Memory gradually returns. Locates a working terminal and hacks into the network. Discovers BIOCOM, the Warden hijack, and classified programs like KDS-Deep and Project Rise. Travels deeper into the facility, following an instinctive pull.

**KDS-Deep Discovery (R4):** Reaches KDS-Deep and finds thousands in hydrostasis. Realizes he was frozen for seven years. Finds his own previous message. Resolves to escape and take as many prisoners with him as possible.

**Original Worldline (R5-R7):** Contacts the player Prisoners through recorded messages. Tests their trustworthiness. Recruits Henriksson, an engineer. Together with the players, they inject a trojan into the Warden mainframe, seizing control of BIOCOM. Schaeffer becomes increasingly unstable and paranoid.

**Alternate Worldline (ALT R4-R6):** A more open-minded Schaeffer communicates with the Conduit, learns the truth about the Collectors and their plan to evacuate survivors to a clean dimension. Works with the Warden rather than against it.

**Rundown 8 Convergence:** OG Schaeffer plans to blow up the Complex with a reactor meltdown. Murders Henriksson when he discovers the engineer's plan to betray him. Alt Schaeffer works with the alt Prisoners toward dimensional escape. OG Schaeffer confronts and kills Alt Schaeffer. The OG Prisoners kill OG Schaeffer in turn.

## Key Relationships

- **The Warden** -- His primary antagonist in the original worldline. The AI entity that controlled him through the implant and used him as a resource. In the alternate worldline, Schaeffer comes to understand the Warden as an extension of the Collectors with a benevolent purpose.
- **Henriksson** -- Engineer ally freed from his control chip with Schaeffer's help. In the original worldline, Henriksson eventually tries to betray Schaeffer to save himself, and Schaeffer murders him.
- **The Prisoners (Quad B452)** -- Bishop, Woods, Hackett, and Dauda. Schaeffer identifies them as the most successful squad and attempts to recruit them. They are his primary tools in both worldlines.
- **The Conduit** -- A humanoid creature created by the Warden using Project Rise technology. OG Schaeffer kills it out of distrust. Alt Schaeffer speaks with it and learns the truth about the Collectors.
- **Igor Rostok (C122)** -- Fellow engineer at Garganta. The two likely worked together before the Legion program claimed Schaeffer.
