## Background

Matthew Aaron is the operations lead for the Daylight Mission, a World Health Organization expedition launched in 2057 to trace the origin of the NAM-V virus that has killed billions and effectively ended human civilization. By the time the mission deploys, the pandemic has been raging for approximately five years, governments have collapsed, and the WHO itself is operating on the ragged edge of institutional survival. The Daylight Mission represents humanity's last organized attempt to understand what destroyed it.

Aaron speaks with military field report cadence -- precise timestamps, measured observations, professional narrative structure -- but with enough personal voice to convey the weight of what he is witnessing. He records his logs as operational updates, documenting logistics, personnel status, and environmental conditions with the disciplined attention of someone who knows these recordings may be the only record of what happened.

The mission Aaron leads is small, under-resourced, and operating in a world that no longer functions. The US government provides no help. The team is stranded in Tampa for two weeks before the Cuban government -- hearing their broadcast -- offers military transport and armed escort. Captain Puente and his Cuban soldiers become the mission's lifeline, a detail Aaron notes with dry irony: "Imagine that. The Cuban Navy land on US soil and no one cares."

## Arc

**April 28, 2057 -- Arrival at Progreso.** Aaron's team reaches the port of Progreso on the Yucatan coast and establishes an operations base in the abandoned cruise terminal. The city is completely empty. Getting there required two weeks stranded in Tampa, rescue by the Cuban Navy from Fort Myers, and a harrowing passage through Florida. Within two days of landing, the team has already lost Dr. Wilkins, who "must have just wandered off." The Cuban military escort reports sightings of coordinated, highly aggressive creatures the soldiers call "chichiricu" -- creatures that vanish before their corpses can be recovered. Aaron notes the creatures but stays focused: "the local fauna is not our mission. Our mission is NAM-V." He finds massive viral loads in the city's corpses and mass cremation sites untouched for 8-10 years, confirming they are on the right track to find the virus's origin.

**May 17, 2057 -- Arrival at Garganta.** Aaron's team descends to what used to be the sea floor, now held back by concrete walls. He describes the entrance to the mine: a drill hole approximately 100 feet across bored into the bedrock, a "bottomless black pit" emitting a smell everyone recognizes but nobody discusses. The surface infrastructure is abandoned but intact -- triage center, military huts, barracks, security checkpoints, holding cells, storage depots, and a fueled Chinook helicopter. No people. No bodies. No signs of evacuation, yet everything has been left behind.

Captain Puente's soldiers refuse to descend further, staying at sea level. Aaron does not blame them. The Daylight Mission team attempts to breach Garganta's security systems -- biometric MFA protocols and multi-tiered network segmentation, all controlled by a program called BIOCOM -- but makes slow progress. Some upper levels are accessible via stairwells, but the elevators and deeper facility remain locked. Aaron reports hearing sounds like "motors or winches" that activate for seconds at a time, go silent for hours, then start again, day and night. These are almost certainly the HSU extraction mechanisms or BIOCOM-controlled systems still operating autonomously years after the complex was abandoned.

**After May 2057 -- Unknown.** According to the lore summary, the Daylight Mission's WHO operatives were intercepted by BIOCOM's defense system during an unauthorized database access attempt. The Warden, still operational and hostile to intruders, likely destroyed the team. Aaron's recordings survive only because BIOCOM's own intercept system logged and filed them.

## Key Relationships

- **Captain Puente** -- Commander of the Cuban military escort. Provides transportation and armed protection from Fort Myers to Progreso, but his men refuse to descend into Garganta. Aaron respects the decision.
- **Dr. Wilkins** -- Team member who disappears shortly after arrival in Progreso. Whether he wandered off voluntarily, was killed by creatures, or succumbed to infection is unknown.
- **Cuban soldiers** -- The armed escort who report "chichiricu" sightings and whose pragmatic refusal to enter the mine contrasts with the scientists' determination to press forward.
- **BIOCOM / The Warden** -- The autonomous system that controls Garganta's security and ultimately intercepts the Daylight Mission. Aaron describes his team's inability to penetrate the biometric systems, unaware that the AI behind them is actively hostile.
