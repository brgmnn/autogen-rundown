## Background

Mia is known from a single psychotherapy session with Dr. August Teale. She is one of the earliest documented cases of what would later be understood as NAM-V parasitic infection manifesting as dermatological and psychological symptoms. Her session is among the most viscerally disturbing documents in the Garganta archives -- a body horror encounter framed as a routine psychiatric referral.

Nothing is known about Mia's role at Garganta, her employee classification, or her life before the session. She exists in the record as a patient, a voice, and a warning that no one heeded.

## Arc

Mia arrives at her session already agitated. She has been referred to Teale -- a psychotherapist -- after reporting to a medical doctor that she saw a bug. She resents the psychiatric referral immediately: "I don't need a shrink." She wants a cream for the itching on her head, which is raw and bleeding from scratching.

The session escalates rapidly. Mia describes waking to feel something crawling on her head. When she turned on the light, it disappeared -- or, as she comes to believe, burrowed into her skin. She can feel it under the surface. She demands that Teale touch her head to confirm what she feels.

The encounter becomes physically violent. Mia grabs Teale's hand, forces it to her head, and hurts him in her desperation to have someone else confirm what she is experiencing. Teale calls for Dr. Banks, trying to break free.

Mia's final words in the transcript are the session's most important: "It's in all of us." This statement, dismissed at the time as the raving of a distressed patient, would prove to be literally true. NAM-V and its parasitic symbiotes were already spreading through the Garganta population. The noises in the walls, the psychological disturbances, the "Garganta Flu" -- Mia was not hallucinating. She was among the first to articulate what was actually happening.

## Key Relationships

- **Dr. August Teale (B023):** Conducting psychotherapist. Mia's session was one of the formative encounters in Teale's escalating horror at what was happening inside the facility.
- **Dr. Banks:** Medical colleague of Teale's, called for assistance when Mia became physically aggressive.
- **The Medical System:** Mia's experience was pathologized as a psychological issue (hence the psychiatric referral) rather than investigated as a physical phenomenon.
