## Background

Ramona, known on-air exclusively as "Mrs. Truth," is the wife of the conspiracy radio host Mr. Truth. She never speaks directly in any broadcast or log. Everything known about her comes through her husband's words, where she exists as a constant, grounding presence -- the person who held him together through decades of off-grid living, government pursuit, and the end of the world.

Mr. Truth and Ramona first moved to Pahrump, Nevada in 2036. He describes the desert community with warmth that clearly reflects her influence: "She loved this town, the people. Desert folk. They are a unique people. Individualists. Good stock. Friendly but not gullible." Pahrump was her place, and by extension, the emotional home base of the Truth broadcast.

Before the apocalypse, Ramona gently challenged her husband's more extreme claims. When Mr. Truth experienced his "false memories" -- clear recollections of the Merida earthquake happening in March rather than April 2049 -- she told him it was his imagination. Whether this was genuine skepticism or protective concern for his mental state is unclear, but it positions her as the pragmatic counterweight to his conspiratorial instincts.

When their compound was raided by FBI and Kovac Defense Services in the early 2050s, Ramona fled with him without hesitation. She traveled the back roads of a collapsing America at his side, past mass graves and burning funeral pyres, through abandoned towns and rotting infrastructure. Mr. Truth established a secondary base called "Castle Truth" where they weathered the worst of the pandemic together.

## Arc

**Pre-2036 -- Early life.** Little is known. She and Mr. Truth were already together before they moved to Pahrump.

**2036 -- Settlement in Pahrump.** The couple arrives in the Nevada desert town, establishing roots in a community of individualists. This becomes their home for nearly two decades.

**Pre-2049 -- The skeptic's role.** When Mr. Truth experiences false memories about the Merida earthquake date, Ramona tells him it is his imagination. She serves as his reality check, the person who keeps his conspiratorial tendencies grounded.

**~2053 -- Flight from the compound.** When KDS and FBI vehicles approach their 40-acre property, Ramona grabs what she can and flees with her husband into the wilderness. There is no recorded hesitation or complaint.

**~2053-2054 -- On the road.** She travels with Mr. Truth through the apocalyptic American landscape. He references her presence throughout these broadcasts: "Mrs. Truth and I are still on the road." She witnesses the same horrors he describes but never speaks.

**~2054 -- Castle Truth.** The couple establishes a fortified secondary location. Mr. Truth credits her implicitly in his return broadcast, speaking of "we" throughout.

**~2057 -- Death in Pahrump.** Ramona dies in or near Pahrump, the town she loved. The exact circumstances are unknown. Mr. Truth's final broadcast from the outskirts of the abandoned town is devastated and incoherent with grief. He slips into third person ("I am glad Mr. Truth is not here to see this"), questions whether he was wrong about something concerning her, and calls her name: "Ramona..." The self-doubt in his voice suggests he may blame himself -- perhaps for dragging her into the wilderness, or for paranoid behavior that damaged their relationship.

## Key Relationships

- **Mr. Truth** -- Her husband. She is his emotional anchor, the hand that holds him up when he cannot stand. Her death appears to fundamentally break his identity: he refers to "Mr. Truth" as a separate person, suggesting the persona cannot survive without her.
- **The Pahrump community** -- She loved the town and its people. Mr. Truth describes the desert folk through the lens of her affection for them.
- **Truth Seekers** -- Mr. Truth's audience knew her as "Mrs. Truth." After the compound raid, concerned messages poured in asking about the couple's safety.
