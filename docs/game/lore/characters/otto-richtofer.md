## Background

Otto Richtofer was a geologist at Garganta, working alongside Dr. Gallus Hammerstein in the early investigation of artifacts recovered from the Inner. His employee ID (B029) places him adjacent to both Hammerstein (B014) and Michel LeFavre (B028) in the specialist tier of the SMC hierarchy, suggesting he was among the original scientific staff brought in for the mining operation.

Richtofer's story is brief but significant: he was the first known person injured by direct contact with an alien artifact, and his incident report provided some of the earliest evidence that the materials recovered from the Inner were not of terrestrial origin.

## Arc

On March 6th, 2052, Richtofer and Hammerstein were analyzing artifacts recovered from site L18-AT06 in the Material Spectroscopy Laboratory. After surface scans proved inconclusive, both scientists agreed that breaking down one of the artifacts for full spectroscopy was necessary. Richtofer placed an artifact into the Jaw Crusher -- a standard piece of geological equipment designed to pulverize rock samples.

The machine had no effect. The artifact remained "intact and untarnished" at full power.

Richtofer then attempted to force the sample deeper into the crusher. The artifact lodged between the crusher plates, causing the mechanism to overload. The hydraulic hoses driving the vibrating plate shattered, and the flying debris struck Richtofer in the left eye, causing a globe luxation -- a displacement of the eyeball from its socket.

The artifact remained unbroken.

Hammerstein's incident report notes that Richtofer's eye was "replaced as quickly as possible" but that vision loss was likely permanent. The report concludes with Hammerstein's opinion that "the material does not appear to be from Earth" -- a professional understatement for what was in fact an extraterrestrial alloy (later identified as containing Cretasium 77a) that humanity had no means to damage.

The artifact samples were sent to SMC headquarters off-site for further analysis. Howard Stokes' subsequent spectroscopy report on similar samples confirmed ~24% unknown material composition, corroborating Hammerstein's assessment.

## Key Relationships

- **Dr. Gallus Hammerstein (B014):** Direct colleague. Hammerstein authored the incident report documenting Richtofer's injury and proposed the initial analysis that led to it.
- **Howard Stokes (B341):** Off-site geologist who received artifacts for spectroscopy analysis after the on-site attempts proved insufficient.
- **Janson Davies (A001):** The Dreyfus executive who, upon reading the incident report, recommended keeping "the circle small" on the findings and dismissed Hammerstein as "a blowhard."
