## Background

Susan Nesbitt was a researcher at Garganta working on botanical genetics, specifically protein sequencing and hydroponics. Her work focused on accelerating plant growth under reduced light conditions -- research that, while seemingly mundane compared to alien artifact analysis, represented critical life-support science for a facility increasingly cut off from the surface world.

Nesbitt is known primarily through her romantic correspondence with James Durant, the scientist who would lead the EF-01 expedition through the Matter Wave Projector. Their email exchange is one of the rare moments of genuine warmth and human connection in the Garganta archives -- a window into the personal lives that existed alongside the professional horror.

## Arc

Nesbitt's correspondence with Durant reveals a scientist in the throes of a breakthrough. Her protein sequencer, applied to Gramineae (grass family) plants using a fungal algorithm, had succeeded in dramatically accelerating growth rates under minimal artificial light. She was ecstatic, her email peppered with typos, exclamation marks, and an exuberant "BLAAAARGH!!!!" that is entirely unlike anything else in the Garganta archives.

She planned to scale her work from grasses to more complex plants -- fruits, vegetables, trees. She had an assistant named Geordie planting new batches and was eager to show Durant the results.

Durant, meanwhile, was in isolation preparing for the EF-01 expedition and desperately wanted Nesbitt to join him. "I don't want to experience this without you," he wrote, signing with "xxx." Nesbitt declined. She had been working for four years to reach this breakthrough and could not abandon it. She affectionately chided Durant for not mentioning her news, told him to bring back specimens, and promised to prepare a new hydroponics lab for his return.

Durant never returned. The EF-01 team was stranded on an alien jungle world with no way back to Garganta. Whether Nesbitt ever learned what happened to him is unknown. Her protein sequencer research, which could have been vital for sustaining a population in isolation, likely died with her or was consumed by the facility's collapse.

## Key Relationships

- **James Durant (B255):** Romantic partner and close colleague. Their correspondence shows mutual affection and shared scientific passion. Durant tried to recruit her for EF-01; she declined.
- **Geordie:** Nesbitt's laboratory assistant, tasked with planting experimental batches of Gramineae under reduced light conditions.
- **Alice:** A member of the EF-01 team whom Durant did not want to be paired with, preferring Nesbitt's company.
- **HeliForm:** A company whose protein sequencer technology Nesbitt used, though she initially considered it "bluster."
