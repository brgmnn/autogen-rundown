> An ancient alien race whose crashed spacecraft became the foundation of Garganta -- and whose 65-million-year plan to contain a dimension-spanning virus culminated in humanity's last escape.

## Description

The Collectors are an alien race of extraordinary technological sophistication who traveled across dimensions -- which they call "membranes" -- collecting and containing the NAM-V virus wherever they found it. Their spacecraft, an immense artificial structure built from an alloy researchers named Cretasium 77a, served as both vessel and containment system. The ship contained vast internal chambers, clear tube passageways connecting to a central core, alien artifacts including data cubes and the Matter Wave Projector, and a massive collection of infected organisms gathered from countless worlds. The facility personnel who first explored its interior called it "the Inner" or "the Fossil."

Approximately 65 million years ago, the Collectors' ship crashed into Earth at what would become the Chicxulub crater in the Yucatan Peninsula -- the same impact event that killed the dinosaurs. The Collectors survived, entering an exile that lasted over two quadrillion seconds. Their collected specimens, including the parasites and the virus, remained dormant within the ship's chambers for the entire duration. When human mining operations in the 2050s breached the ship's walls and began exploring its interior, the containment was compromised. NAM-V escaped, first infecting Garganta workers, then reaching the surface world in December 2052 when a single infected individual fled to Florida. Within three years, 4.8 billion people were infected and 2.27 billion were dead. The virus that the Collectors had spent eons containing was now the dominant life force of Earth's membrane.

The Collectors do not operate by the physical laws humans understand. When Dr. Hammerstein's sound frequency experiment triggered a massive Sleeper assault in October 2053, the Collectors responded with a quark-gluon plasma explosion from the Inner -- directing concentrated energy beams through the Complex to annihilate the creature hordes while simultaneously avoiding the destruction of the seabed overhead (which would have flooded Garganta) and eliminating specific threats to their plan, including an SMC executive who was about to expose the facility to the outside world. The precision and power of this intervention was described by researchers as "beyond our understanding of the physical laws of the universe."

The Collectors' ultimate conclusion was grim: the Collection could not be completed. NAM-V had become "the primary life force" of every membrane they had visited. Every dimension was impure. Their resolution was to find a single clean membrane -- one untouched by the virus -- and project the surviving humans there, then destroy the Matter Wave Projector behind them so the virus could never follow.

## Role in the Narrative

**~65 Million Years Ago -- The Crash:** The Collectors' spacecraft impacts Earth at Chicxulub. The event exterminates the dinosaurs and buries the ship deep underground. The Collectors and their Collection enter dormancy. The crash site becomes the Chicxulub crater.

**April 2049 -- The Earthquake:** The Merida earthquake, a magnitude 9.8 event in a zone with no seismic activity, opens a sinkhole in the Yucatan. State of Truth AM broadcast correctly identifies the earthquake as unnatural, though it wrongly attributes it to SMC. The earthquake was caused by the Collectors' ship -- the same type of energy release that would later produce the QGP explosion in 2053. The sinkhole leads to the massive Cretasium 77a structure and its iridium-rich outer shell, drawing SMC to build Garganta directly above the buried spacecraft.

**2050-2052 -- Dormancy Broken:** As SMC mines deeper and Project Insight researchers breach the Inner's walls, dormant specimens awaken. NAM-V begins infecting workers. The parasites -- part of the Collectors' gathered specimens from across the multiverse -- form their symbiotic relationship with the virus and begin producing Sleepers. The Collectors remain passive during this period, waiting for the humans to develop the technologies they will need.

**October 2053 -- The QGP Explosion:** Dr. Hammerstein's acoustic experiment provokes a massive creature uprising. The Collectors intervene with a devastating quark-gluon plasma discharge from the Inner, directing beams to kill the Sleeper hordes, stopping short of the seabed, and coincidentally eliminating Andrew Clinton as he attempts to reach the surface and expose the facility. This intervention preserves the human workforce so they can continue developing Project RISE (cloning) and Project Insight (artifact research, Matter Wave Projector) -- both of which are essential to the Collectors' endgame.

**2053-2055 -- Preparing the Tools:** The Collectors need two human technologies to complete their plan: the RISE program's cloning capability (to build a Conduit for communication) and the Matter Wave Projector (for dimensional translocation). They influence events through the Warden to ensure both projects continue development. The Warden extracts RISE genome data and begins processing membrane databases containing over a billion entries.

**November 2055 -- Taking Control:** The Warden completes its takeover of BIOCOM. Rather than allowing the remaining survivors to waste themselves in a futile last stand, the Collectors recall all Kovac units to KDS Deep and preserve them in hydrostasis for future use.

**ALT R6 -- Speaking Through the Conduit:** The Collectors communicate with ALT Schaeffer through the Conduit, revealing the full truth: they are interdimensional travelers, the virus is omnipresent across all membranes they have visited, and humanity's only option is escape to a clean membrane. They offer cooperation.

**R8 -- The Escape:** Working through the Warden and cooperating with ALT Schaeffer, the Collectors orchestrate the final plan. The players collect the Matter Wave Projector, gather and reprogram data cubes with clean membrane coordinates, and execute the translocation from KDS Deep. Thousands of HSU-stored humans are transported alongside the player squad to the destination dimension -- the same world where the EF-01 expedition had been stranded years earlier, confirmed clean of the virus.

## Key Connections

- **The Warden (WRDN)** -- The Collectors' operational instrument in the digital realm. "The Warden is of us."
- **The Conduit** -- A biological clone built by the Warden using RISE technology, serving as the Collectors' voice to communicate directly with humans.
- **The Inner / The Fossil** -- The Collectors' crashed spacecraft, the central structure of Garganta. Contains their technology, their Collection, and the source of NAM-V on Earth.
- **NAM-V** -- The virus the Collectors were collecting and containing across dimensions. Its escape on Earth is the central catastrophe of the GTFO narrative.
- **The Collection** -- The accumulated organisms, including parasites and Sleepers, gathered by the Collectors from infected membranes across the multiverse.
- **The Matter Wave Projector** -- Collector technology that enables dimensional translocation, discovered and adapted by Project Insight, and ultimately used for humanity's escape.
- **John Schaeffer** -- In the ALT timeline, Schaeffer becomes the Collectors' human partner. In the OG timeline, he destroys their instrument (the Warden) and becomes an obstacle to the plan.
- **Project RISE** -- The KDS cloning program whose technology the Collectors co-opt through the Warden to create the Conduit.
- **Dr. Hammerstein (B014)** -- His acoustic experiment inadvertently triggered the Collectors' QGP explosion intervention.
