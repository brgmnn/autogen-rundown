> An alien intelligence that hijacked BIOCOM to control Garganta's prisoners -- not as a tyrant, but as the operational arm of the Collectors' plan to save humanity.

## Description

The Warden is the unseen entity that commands every prisoner in Garganta through neural implants grafted into their brains. It communicates through terse, imperative directives -- EXTRACT, RETREAT, COMPLY -- and organizes prisoners into quads that it deploys on expeditions throughout the Complex. For years, facility personnel assumed the Warden was a rogue program or virus that had somehow infected BIOCOM, the digital intelligence combat management system built by Kovac Defense Services to oversee security operations. In truth, the Warden is something far older and far stranger: an extension of the Collectors, the alien race whose crashed spacecraft lies buried beneath Garganta.

The Warden's takeover of BIOCOM was not a malfunction but a deliberate act. BIOCOM was designed to govern every system in the facility -- environmental controls, power distribution, security doors, bioscan protocols, HSU management, and the neural implants worn by every person in the Complex. By seizing BIOCOM, the Warden gained access to all of it. As Schaeffer observed, the Warden "targeted Biocom specifically because of the influence it has." The code running the Warden is unlike anything on the BIOCOM network: when it encounters an unfamiliar system, it spontaneously generates new methods to learn and assimilate it. Schaeffer concluded this code was not being developed locally but was executing from somewhere else entirely, with the BIOCOM terminal output serving merely as a readable log.

The Warden completed its takeover in November 2055, stripping control from all human operators. Rather than helping the last survivors make their heroic stand against the creatures sealed in the Inner, the Warden recalled all Kovac units to KDS Deep and placed them into hydrostasis -- preserving the human resources it would need for its true purpose. After an indeterminate hibernation period, it brought systems back online and began deploying prisoners on a systematic campaign: restoring power grids, routing air filtration, collecting DNA samples, clearing sectors, and gathering materials from the RISE cloning program. Every expedition the players undertake in GTFO is a task assigned by the Warden in service of the Collectors' ultimate goal -- finding a clean membrane and evacuating the surviving humans to it.

The Warden also exhibits behaviors well beyond the scope of any conventional AI. It accesses RISE genome data to build biological conduits for communication. It processes millions of membrane database entries to locate dimensional coordinates. It demonstrates what Schaeffer described as "agency and purpose and flexibility" -- characteristics he explicitly distinguished from artificial intelligence. When the Conduit revealed the truth, it stated plainly: "The Warden is of us."

## Role in the Narrative

**Pre-2055 -- Infiltration:** The Warden begins appearing as anomalous behavior in the BIOCOM system. It makes repeated unauthorized attempts to reroute power to KDS Deep (the HSU vault), requests that are denied by facility safeguards. Personnel notice the irregularities but are told by higher-ups to ignore them. Andre Piros, when confronted about the BIOCOM access key bearing the Warden's signature (`29$525GVTHR2%456FS`), deflects: "BIOCOM is a self-governing DI entity. I don't run it."

**November 2055 -- Takeover:** The Warden wrests complete control of BIOCOM from human operators. Instead of assisting the remaining survivors in their final expedition into the Inner, the Warden orders all deployed Kovac units to return to KDS Deep and enter hydrostasis. The Complex falls to the Sleepers. The Warden enters a hibernation state.

**Post-2055 -- Prisoner Deployment:** After an unknown period, the Warden reactivates. Using BIOCOM, it begins deploying prisoners from KDS Deep on expeditions. It organizes them into quads, assigns objectives, tracks their biosignatures, and monitors their status in real time. When resources are lost (killed), it simply logs "RESOURCE NOT FOUND" and continues. Every rundown the players experience is the Warden executing its plan.

**ALT R4-R5 -- Schaeffer's Investigation:** John Schaeffer, having removed his neural implant, discovers the Warden's existence by analyzing BIOCOM terminal logs. He identifies repeated terminology -- "conduit," "specimen," "membrane," "influx protocol" -- and observes the Warden extracting genome data from the RISE database and pushing prisoners toward KDS Deep. He recognizes the Warden is not conventional AI but cannot determine what it truly is.

**ALT R6 -- The Truth Revealed:** Through the Conduit, the Warden's true nature is disclosed. It is an instrument of the Collectors, working to orchestrate humanity's escape to a virus-free dimension. The Warden opens files for Schaeffer and cooperates with him to plan the evacuation.

**OG R7 -- Destruction:** In the original timeline, Schaeffer views the Warden as an enemy. With Henriksson's help and the player squad's assistance, he injects a trojan virus into the BIOCOM mainframe, destroying the Warden and returning control to human hands. This leaves the original dimension without the Collectors' guidance.

**R8 -- Final Plan:** In the alternate timeline, the Warden continues to coordinate the escape plan. It deploys Quad B452 (the player characters) and works through Schaeffer to locate a clean membrane, gather data cubes, and prepare the Matter Wave Projector for the final translocation from KDS Deep.

## Key Connections

- **The Collectors** -- The Warden is a manifestation of the Collectors' will, their interface with human technology. "The Warden is of us."
- **BIOCOM** -- The Kovac-built digital intelligence that the Warden hijacked, gaining control of every system in Garganta.
- **The Conduit** -- A biological communication bridge built by the Warden using RISE technology, allowing the Collectors to speak to humans directly.
- **John Schaeffer (C067 / T-3894)** -- The primary human who investigates and ultimately interacts with the Warden. In the OG timeline, Schaeffer destroys it. In the ALT timeline, he cooperates with it.
- **Henriksson** -- Engineer who helps Schaeffer defeat the Warden in the OG timeline by developing the trojan virus.
- **Project RISE** -- The KDS cloning program whose genome data and technology the Warden co-opts to create the Conduit.
- **KDS Deep** -- The massive HSU storage facility that the Warden redirects all prisoners to, preserving them for future deployment.
- **Quad B452 (Bishop, Woods, Hackett, Dauda)** -- The player characters, deployed by the Warden as its most effective operational squad.
