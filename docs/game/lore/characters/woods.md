## Background

Isiah Woods was a US military operative with an extraordinary combat record, including the legendary "Miracle Mile" in Turkey. When Erzurum fell to rebels, Woods was spared. He spent twenty-six days behind enemy lines, crossed the DMZ under sniper fire, and walked blind through a minefield -- all without injury. He attributed his survival not to luck but to divine purpose: a personal message from God that he had a duty to fulfill.

That duty, as Woods understood it, led him to kill. At Larnaca, he executed several marines he considered corrupt and evil -- "apostles of the devil" who had "strayed from the path." He offered them a chance to hear the word before their judgment, but they refused. Woods showed no remorse; he saw himself as an instrument of divine justice in a world already condemned. When told he might face the death penalty, he responded simply: "You cannot kill me. It is not in his plan."

Rather than execution, Woods was sent to the Legion program -- a "new program, designed for people just like you." Woods accepted this calmly, even with foreknowledge: "I knew before you did. I am going where he needs me." His final words to his interrogator were prophetic: "When the plague comes, I shall be rescued from the lion's mouth, and he will lead us from the darkness." He was an actual prisoner sent to the procedure by force, though he went with the conviction that it was God's will.

Within the Legion program, Woods adapted to his conditioning with unsettling composure. After nearly two years of service, he was evaluated for signs of reverting to "old behavior patterns" -- specifically, indications of religious faith. KDS policy prohibited operatives from holding any belief systems. Woods responded to probing questions with careful deflection, repeating programmed responses ("I am proven in combat. It is what I train for") while maintaining an undercurrent of spiritual awareness his handlers could not fully erase.

## Arc

**Military Service (pre-2050s):** Distinguished combat record in Turkey. The "Miracle Mile" becomes legendary. Develops absolute faith that God is guiding him personally.

**The Killings at Larnaca:** Executes several marines he considers corrupt. No remorse. Frames it as holy duty. Captured and interrogated. Prophesies the coming plague and his own rescue from darkness.

**Legion Induction:** Sent to the Legion program as punishment. Accepts it willingly, believing it is part of God's plan. Undergoes hydrostasis and memory conditioning.

**Operative Service (~2 years):** Performs as a competent KSO operative. Maintains a careful balance -- obeying commands, passing evaluations, but preserving an inner awareness that his handlers find troubling. Deflects questions about faith. Asks "Have I been reset before?" -- suggesting awareness of the cycle.

**KDS-Deep and Dauda:** When the facility falls, Woods is among the operatives retreating to KDS-Deep. He intervenes to save Dr. Abeo Dauda from being shot by Hackett, ordering "Exfil, soldier" and then directing Dauda into an HSU: "Get in... Or you can die, brother." This act of mercy -- possibly the only one witnessed during the evacuation -- results in Dauda being assigned KSO designation T-3701 and eventually joining their quad.

**Quad B452 (~2063):** Assigned by the Warden/BIOCOM system alongside Bishop, Hackett, and Dauda. Status: Seek and Destroy. Deployed on expeditions into the Complex.

## Key Relationships

- **Hackett (O-4711)** -- Fellow Quad B452 member. Woods overrides Hackett's aggression at KDS-Deep to save Dauda. Their dynamic is one of contrasts: Hackett's cynical pragmatism against Woods's quiet conviction.
- **Dauda (T-3701)** -- Woods saves Dauda's life at KDS-Deep, ordering him into an HSU. This act of compassion under protocol-breaking circumstances is unique among the KSO evacuees.
- **Bishop (GO-1395)** -- Fellow Quad B452 member. Both were prisoners sent to Legion by force.
- **KDS Handlers** -- Woods's relationship with the Legion program is one of surface compliance masking spiritual resilience. His handlers are visibly uncomfortable with his faith but unable to fully erase it.
